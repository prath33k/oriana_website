Jam

Just-A-Minute (or JAM) is an all round fun event that is all about the control of the mind over the mouth. Can you make it through sixty seconds of nonstop talking without hesitation, repetition or deviation? Or will the sheer pressure make you crumble and have your competitors pounce on you in an instant?

  • Objective

  • The objective of the game is to speak for the maximum duration in a round of sixty seconds. Once a participant is given an opportunity to speak all other participants will try to raise objections on numerous counts. If the objection is found to be valid then the one who objects becomes the speaker. Otherwise the speaker remains unchanged. All this will be conducted by the JAM master.

  • Rules

  • 1. The JAM master is your god. No questions or arguments are to be made once the final decision has been made.

  • 2. To object, slam the table and say"JAM". The JAM master decides when you are allowed to put forth your objection.

  • 3. You get a penalty if your competitors object on any of the following counts:

  • a) You shutter, stammer, and generally indulge in time wasting tactics. These include efforts to buy time like interspersing your talk with "uhh", "aah" etc.

  • b) Early and late start.

  • c) Speaking too fast or too slow.

  • d) Grammatical errors.

  • e) Undue stress on words, undue emphasis on the sentences.

  • f) Repetition of a previously used idea.

  • g) Not following the 'random rules' as set by the JAM master.

  • 4. The total time is sixty seconds for each round.

  • 5. Points are given in the following manner:

  • a) Positive points for every second that you speak.

  • b) Positive points for every correct objection.

  • c) Negative points for every incorrect objection.

  • d) Bonus points for being the last speaker.

  • 6. The winner is the person with the maximum number of points at the end.

  • 7. The last point is an extension of the first point. As the JAM master is god he can suspend any of the above rules before the start of a round, can add new rules for a round, or conduct a qualifying/elimination round. Any such changes shall be informed to the participants.
